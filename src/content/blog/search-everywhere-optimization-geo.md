## Search Everywhere Optimization nima?

Search Everywhere Optimization — odamlar javobni endi faqat Google'dan emas, balki ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews, Reddit, Quora, YouTube, podkastlar va ijtimoiy tarmoqlardan izlayotgani uchun brendni **hamma joyda ko'rinadigan** qilish strategiyasi.

Avval rasm sodda edi: Google'da top'ga chiqasiz — tamom. Endi "discovery" (brendni topish jarayoni) tarqoq holatga keldi. Bir odam ertalab Google'da qidiradi, tushlikda ChatGPT'dan so'raydi, kechqurun YouTube'da sharh ko'radi, qaror qabul qilishdan oldin Reddit'ga kirib real tajribani o'qiydi. Siz bu zanjirning faqat bitta bo'g'inida bo'lsangiz — yo'qotasiz.

[AEO](/blog/aeo-nima-chatgpt-davri) va [SEO asoslari](/blog/seo-nima-ozbekiston-2026) bu rasmnи bir qism — bu post ularni butun manzaraga ulaydi.

Va eng muhim o'zgarish AI tomonida: **Meta AI 1 mlrd+ oylik foydalanuvchi bilan amalda 2-eng mashhur LLM** bo'lib turibdi. Ya'ni odamlar ChatGPT'dan tashqari yana bir necha AI platformasidan kundalik javob olishadi.

## SEO / AEO / GEO — farqi nimada?

Poydevor uchchovida bir xil: ishonchli, strukturali, original kontent. Farq **scoreboard'da** — nimani o'lchaysiz va qayerda g'olib bo'lasiz.

| | SEO | AEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|---|

E'tibor bering: bular bir-birini almashtirmaydi. SEO — fundament, AEO va GEO — ustiga qo'shiladigan qatlamlar. Eski KPIlar (reyting, CTR, trafik) o'rniga endi **share of visibility** (ko'rinish ulushi) va **sentiment** (qanday ohangda eslanasiz) muhim.

## Texnik tugun: JavaScript va SSR

Bu — Next.js, React yoki shunga o'xshash JS-og'ir saytlarda ishlaydiganlar uchun eng muhim texnik nuqta.

LLM botlari — GPTBot (OpenAI), ClaudeBot (Anthropic) va boshqalar — JavaScript'ni **Googlebot kabi yaxshi o'qiy olmaydi**. Googlebot yillar davomida JS render qilishni o'rgangan; AI botlari esa ko'pincha shunchaki dastlabki HTML'ni oladi.

Bu nimani anglatadi? Agar saytingizda muhim kontent — narx sahifasi, bosh sahifa, FAQ — faqat brauzerda JavaScript yuklaganidan keyin paydo bo'lsa, AI uchun u **ko'rinmas** qoladi. AI uni o'qiy olmaydi, demak javobida ishlatmaydi.

Yechim:

- **SSR (Server-Side Rendering)** yoki **SSG (Static Site Generation)** bilan muhim kontentni server tomonda render qiling.
- AI botga **to'liq HTML** yetib borishini tekshiring — sahifaning JavaScript'siz ko'rinishini ko'ring (masalan, brauzerda JS'ni o'chirib yoki `curl` bilan tekshirib).
- Narx, asosiy ta'rif, FAQ kabi "AI iqtibos qiladigan" qismlar albatta HTML'da bo'lsin, JS'ning ortida emas.

Ironiya shundaki, saytni "zamonaviy" qilaman deb og'ir JS'ga o'tkazish ko'pincha uni AI uchun ko'rinmas qiladi.

## Backlink emas — mention va sentiment

Mana eng katta falsafiy farq. Klassik SEO backlink ustiga qurilgan edi. AI uchun esa boshqacha:

- **Backlink AI uchun kamroq ahamiyatli.** Foydali, lekin asosiy emas.
- **Tabiiy mention (eslatilish) — undan kam emas, ba'zan ko'proq qimmatli.** Yuqori obro'li joyda brendingiz tabiiy tilga olinsa — link bo'lmasa ham — AI buni kuchli signal deb oladi.
- **AI hatto linksiz ham brendni mavzuga bog'lay oladi.** Agar sohangizdagi ishonchli nashrlar sizni "shu mavzu bo'yicha gapiradigan kishi" sifatida eslab tursa, AI sizni o'sha mavzuga ulaydi.

Va aniq maslahat: **Domain Authority degan ko'rsatkichni butunlay unuting.** Bu Moz o'ylab topgan uchinchi tomon raqami, Google rasman ishlatmaydi, LLM'lar esa umuman e'tibor bermaydi. Muhimi — sanoatingizdagi ishonchli saytlar va nashrlar sizni mavzuga oid tarzda gapirishi va qanday **sentiment** (ijobiy/salbiy ohang) bilan gapirishi.

## Nima ishlaydi

**1. Information gain — yangi narsa.** Boshqalar aytmagan: o'z tadqiqotingiz, datangiz, real tajribangiz. AI dam ololmaydi, mahsulotni o'z qo'li bilan sinab ko'rolmaydi — shuning uchun Reddit va YouTube'dagi real tajribaga asoslangan kontent g'olib bo'ladi. Sizning afzalligingiz — AI takrorlay olmaydigan tajriba.

**2. Yechim sahifalarini "marketing debt"dan tozalash.** "Biz eng zo'rmiz" o'rniga miqdoriy yozing: "X% samaradorlik beradi", "Y soat tejaydi". AI maqtovni emas, raqamni iqtibos qiladi.

**3. Uch ustun: Content → Technical → Trust/Authority.** Ketma-ketlik shu: avval qadrli kontent, keyin uni AI o'qiy oladigan texnik holatga keltirish, oxirida obro' va ishonch tarqatish. Qisqa shior: **"Odam uchun yoz, AI uchun qadoqla."**

**4. GEO SEO'dan tezroq natija beradi.** Feedback tez keladi — AI'da o'zgarish haftalar ichida ko'rinadi, oylar emas. Demak tez pivot qilsa bo'ladi: ishlamagan yondashuvni tezda almashtirasiz.

**4. Eng nozik nüans: "written by AI" hissi.** Odam kontentni "AI yozgan" deb his qilsa, ishonchi uziladi — "AI yozgan" yorlig'i engagementni keskin tushirgan. Texnik jihatdan AI yordam bersa ham, natija inson ovozida yangrashi kerak.

A friend had built his pricing page as a slick React component — animations, an interactive calculator, all of it. We asked ChatGPT "how much does [his service] cost". The AI replied: "the price isn't listed openly." But the price is right there on that page — it just appears after JavaScript loads. The AI bot didn't wait for JavaScript: it saw empty HTML and left.

This isn't about one page. The point is — search isn't just Google anymore. People look for answers in a dozen places, and in each one your brand is either present or absent.

## What is Search Everywhere Optimization?

Search Everywhere Optimization is the strategy of making your brand **visible everywhere** — because people now look for answers not only on Google but on ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, AI Overviews, Reddit, Quora, YouTube, podcasts and social networks.

The picture used to be simple: rank on Google, done. Now "discovery" (how people find a brand) has scattered. Someone searches Google in the morning, asks ChatGPT at lunch, watches a YouTube review in the evening, and before deciding, reads real experiences on Reddit. If you're present in only one link of that chain — you lose.

[AEO](/blog/aeo-nima-chatgpt-davri) and [SEO basics](/blog/seo-nima-ozbekiston-2026) are parts of this picture — this post ties them into the full landscape.

## Where search scattered — the numbers

The best evidence is daily search volume. These figures show how search has spread across platforms:

| Platform | Daily search volume (approx.) |
|---|---|
| Google | ~13.7B |
| Instagram | ~6.5B |
| YouTube | ~3.3B |
| TikTok | 1B+ |

Google still leads, but it isn't the only one. Search volume on Instagram and YouTube is already in the billions — these aren't just "social networks", they're search engines.

And the most important shift is on the AI side: **Meta AI, with 1B+ monthly users, is effectively the 2nd most popular LLM.** Meaning people get daily answers from several AI platforms beyond ChatGPT.

The conclusion is simple: you need to be everywhere, not only on Google.

## The click is disappearing — but revenue can grow

This is the part that causes the most confusion. Traffic is dropping, but that's not bad news. Look at the numbers:

| Metric | Figure | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Zero-click searches | 60% | Forbes |
| AI Overviews impact on organic CTR | −58% | Ahrefs |
| Organic traffic forecast by 2028 | another −50% | Gartner |

At first glance — disaster. But there's a subtlety: people who read the AI answer and then **still come to your site are "more ready."** They've already got the basics, compared options, made half the decision. So conversion is higher.

So the goal shifts: it's no longer a fight for the **#1 position**. It's about becoming a **trusted, cited brand**. Fewer clicks, but higher-quality clicks.

## SEO / AEO / GEO — what's the difference?

The foundation is the same for all three: trustworthy, structured, original content. The difference is the **scoreboard** — what you measure and where you win.

| | SEO | AEO | GEO |
|---|---|---|---|
| Definition | Ranking in search | Being "the answer" | Getting cited on AI platforms |
| Main arena | Google results | AI Overviews, answer boxes | ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Claude |
| Old KPI | Ranking, CTR, traffic | Featured snippet, answer block | — |
| New KPI | — | Share of visibility | Citation, sentiment |
| Goal | Get the click | Be the answer | Be named as a source |

Note: these don't replace one another. SEO is the foundation; AEO and GEO are layers on top. Instead of the old KPIs (ranking, CTR, traffic), what matters now is **share of visibility** and **sentiment** (the tone in which you're mentioned).

## The technical knot: JavaScript and SSR

This is the most important technical point for anyone working on JS-heavy sites — Next.js, React or similar.

LLM crawlers — GPTBot (OpenAI), ClaudeBot (Anthropic) and others — **don't read JavaScript as well as Googlebot**. Googlebot spent years learning to render JS; AI bots often just take the initial HTML.

What does that mean? If important content — pricing, homepage, FAQ — only appears after JavaScript loads in the browser, it stays **invisible** to AI. The AI can't read it, so it won't use it in answers.

The fix:

- Render important content server-side with **SSR (Server-Side Rendering)** or **SSG (Static Site Generation)**.
- Verify the AI bot receives **full HTML** — check how the page looks without JavaScript (disable JS in the browser, or test with `curl`).
- Make sure the parts AI tends to cite — price, core definitions, FAQ — live in the HTML, not behind JS.

The irony: "modernizing" a site by going heavy on JS often makes it invisible to AI.

## Not backlinks — mentions and sentiment

Here's the biggest philosophical shift. Classic SEO was built on backlinks. For AI it's different:

- **Backlinks matter less to AI.** Useful, but not primary.
- **A natural mention is no less valuable — sometimes more.** When your brand is mentioned naturally in a high-authority place — even without a link — AI treats it as a strong signal.
- **AI can connect a brand to a topic even without a link.** If trusted publications in your field reference you as "someone who speaks on this topic", AI ties you to that topic.

And a concrete piece of advice: **forget the Domain Authority metric entirely.** It's a third-party number invented by Moz; Google doesn't officially use it, and LLMs ignore it completely. What matters is whether trusted sites and publications in your industry talk about you in a topically relevant way — and with what **sentiment** (positive/negative tone).

## How AI picks content — order of importance

What does AI weigh, and what does it ignore? Here's the priority order:

| Level | What |
|---|---|
| 🟢 High priority | Trust, brand mentions, sentiment, authority, original research/data, structured content, named authors (EEAT) |
| 🟡 Low priority | Page speed, backlinks (don't neglect them, but they're not primary) |
| 🔴 Ignored | Thin pages, content built only on keyword volume, content with no point of view |

The key takeaway: AI looks at **insight and experience**, not **volume and keywords**.

## What works

**1. Information gain — something new.** What others haven't said: your own research, your data, your real experience. AI can't take a break or test a product with its own hands — which is why experience-based content on Reddit and YouTube wins. Your advantage is experience AI can't replicate.

**2. Clean "marketing debt" off your solution pages.** Instead of "we're the best", write quantitatively: "delivers X% efficiency", "saves Y hours". AI cites the number, not the praise.

**3. Three pillars: Content → Technical → Trust/Authority.** In that order: first valuable content, then make it technically readable for AI, finally spread authority and trust. Short motto: **"Write for humans, package for AI."**

**4. GEO gives results faster than SEO.** Feedback comes quickly — changes show up in AI within weeks, not months. So you can pivot fast: swap out an approach that isn't working right away.

## What doesn't work

**1. Mass-producing AI content.** Lily Ray's analysis made it clear — the AI "slop" traffic curve looks like this:

| Period | Result |
|---|---|
| 3-4 months | Traffic rises |
| Month 6 | Collapses |
| After | Falls below baseline |

A short-term win — a long-term loss.

**2. Publishing a lot of content without authority.** Without amplification (digital PR, earning citations), no matter how much you write, AI won't mark you as "trusted".

**3. Old "vanity" reporting.** Looking only at traffic — now a metric that no longer guides decisions.

**4. The subtlest nuance: the "written by AI" feeling.** When people sense content was "written by AI", their trust breaks — an "AI-written" label has sharply cut engagement. Even if AI helps technically, the result must sound in a human voice.

## A new measurement system

Build your dashboard **top-down** — the old system was inverted (traffic first); the new one starts from the business:

1. **Business KPIs** (at the top) — leads, sales, revenue.
2. **Influence metrics** (middle) — AI citation and visibility.
3. **Foundation metrics** (at the bottom) — traffic, impressions.

Track six additional directions:

- Branded search growth (people searching for your name)
- Assisted conversions
- Share of voice across platforms
- Returning-visit growth
- **AI citation visibility** — not just "do you appear" but **in what sentiment**. Real example: a brand recommended in the US but warned against in Germany — fixed with localization.
- Conversion quality

The most important rule: **teams must not be siloed.** If SEO, content and PR work separately, GEO won't work — they're all one strategy.

## Closing

Search has scattered, and it won't re-consolidate into one place. Google still matters, but it's no longer the only arena — ChatGPT, AI Overviews, Reddit, YouTube and social networks answer the same person's same question in different places.
