OpenAI released its own data: over half of ChatGPT's active users now write in a language other than English. And on that list of "other languages" — Uzbek is there, among the fastest-growing.

## What happened

According to statistics OpenAI released via its Signals program, the share of ChatGPT's active users writing in a language other than English has crossed 50% — the most common being Spanish, Portuguese, and Arabic. Search Engine Journal reported this on June 30, 2026.

The most interesting part is the growth ranking. Among languages with 1M+ users, **the three with the largest growth are Uzbek, Kazakh, and Burmese**. OpenAI also noted that the fastest growth is happening in Asia and Africa, especially in lower-to-middle-income countries.

## The numbers — what's known, what isn't

Let's be transparent: OpenAI hasn't published an exact percentage or user count for Uzbek — only which languages sit at the top of the growth ranking. So there's no precise answer yet to "how many million Uzbeks use ChatGPT." But the direction itself is a signal: the company called this out specifically, not as a footnote.
